This episode explores the Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI), a scientific effort spanning decades to detect signals from intelligent alien life. Astronomers note that current search efforts have only examined a small fraction of available data—roughly equivalent to a hot tub of water compared to the ocean—and are expanding beyond traditional radio signal analysis to include optical telescopes and other methods to search a much larger volume of space.

Ira Flatow · Feb 23 · 12 min

Despite advances in understanding mental illness biology, no blood test or brain scan can definitively diagnose depression, anxiety, PTSD, or other psychiatric disorders. The upcoming DSM revision will include biomarkers, but Dr. John Crystal from Yale explains that current biomarker research is largely aspirational rather than clinically ready.
