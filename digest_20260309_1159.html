MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: What crocodile bones teach us about dinosaurs
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Mar 09 · 11 min

Researchers studying Nile crocodiles at a reptile center in South Africa discovered that crocodile bones form multiple growth marks per year rather than one annual ring, challenging the traditional method of estimating dinosaur ages by counting rings. The team, led by paleobiologist Anousia Chinsami-Taran, injected young crocodiles with antibiotic markers and later analyzed their bone structure, finding up to five growth rings in two-year-old crocodiles. This suggests that previous dinosaur age estimates may have been overestimates, though scientists caution that growth ring reliability varies across species and the full picture remains incomplete.

MEDIUM REACH Science Friday: The Surprising Science Of Why Sneakers Squeak
Ira Flatow · Mar 09 · 14 min

This Science Friday episode explores the physics behind squeaking basketball shoes through experimental analysis. Researchers at Harvard discovered that shoe squeaks result from supersonic slip pulses that travel through the shoe sole, creating wrinkles that move faster than sound. Surprisingly, the phenomenon shares similarities with earthquake ruptures, and electrostatic discharge (similar to static electricity) triggers these slip pulses by creating localized heat and pressure changes. The study was published in Nature and demonstrates how seemingly simple everyday questions can reveal complex and unexpected physics.
