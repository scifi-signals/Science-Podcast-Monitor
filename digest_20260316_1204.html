HIGH REACH Radiolab: Return of the Flesh-Eaters
Lulu Miller, Latif Nasser · Mar 13 · 42 min

This episode traces the history of the New World screwworm, a flesh-eating parasitic fly that devastated livestock across North America. It chronicles Edward Nippling's mid-20th century innovation of using radiation-sterilized males to suppress screwworm populations—a technique called the Sterile Insect Technique (SIT)—which successfully eradicated the pest from the U.S. and Central America. However, the episode reveals that screwworms have recently breached the biological barrier maintained at the Panama-Colombia border and are advancing northward, raising urgent questions about whether humanity should use gene drives to permanently eliminate the species rather than perpetually manage it.

Dr. Peter Attia provides an in-depth analysis of obicetrapib, a CETP inhibitor being developed to lower LDL cholesterol and ApoB. Attia emphasizes this is a biomarker study requiring validation through cognitive outcomes trials before clinical recommendations can be made.
