MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: We saved gray whales from extinction. Why are so many dying again?
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Mar 11 · 13 min

Gray whales, once hunted to near extinction, were successfully recovered through international protection efforts and removed from the endangered species list in 1994. However, since 1999, scientists have documented recurring cycles of mass die-offs affecting 20-30% of the population, followed by recoveries. Research by Joshua Stewart and colleagues identified that these boom-bust cycles correlate with fluctuations in benthic crustacean biomass in Arctic feeding grounds, where whales consume their annual food supply. The study reveals that populations at carrying capacity are highly sensitive to environmental prey availability variations, and the current ongoing decline since 2019 appears driven by climate change impacts on Arctic ecosystems.
