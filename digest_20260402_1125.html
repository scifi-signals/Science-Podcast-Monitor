Planetary scientists Dr. Alan Stern and Dr. Amanda Bosch argue that the IAU's definition—which excludes objects that haven't cleared their orbital neighborhoods—is scientifically arbitrary and that most planetary scientists now classify Pluto as a planet based on a simpler definition: objects large enough to be rounded by self-gravity but not massive enough to ignite fusion. The episode explores the controversy surrounding voting on scientific definitions and how discoveries from the New Horizons mission in 2015 revealed Pluto to be a geologically complex world with mountains, glaciers, an atmosphere, and moons.
