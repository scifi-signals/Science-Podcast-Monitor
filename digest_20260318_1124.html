Toxicologist Peter Ross explains that the smoke and resulting rainfall contain hazardous compounds including sulfur oxides, nitrogen oxides, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, benzene, and toluene, which pose immediate respiratory threats and long-term contamination risks to soil, groundwater, and drinking water supplies.
