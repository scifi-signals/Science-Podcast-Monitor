MEDIUM REACH Nature Podcast: How earthquakes and lightning help explain squeaky sneakers
Springer Nature · Feb 25 · 20 min

This Nature podcast episode covers two main scientific topics: the physics of squeaky shoes and human hippocampal neurogenesis. Researchers at Harvard and University of Nottingham discovered that shoe squeaks result from 'opening pulses'—waves of detachment traveling at the speed of sound through rubber—triggered by surface patterns and electrostatic charging (tribocharging), mechanisms similar to those in earthquakes. The second segment reports findings from University of Illinois Chicago showing that hippocampal neurogenesis (generation of new neurons) occurs in human brains and is significantly elevated in 'super-agers' with preserved cognitive function, with differences linked to epigenetic alterations, suggesting potential targets for therapeutic intervention.
