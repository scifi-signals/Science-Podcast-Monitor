HIGH REACH Hidden Brain: When It's Okay to Lie
Shankar Vedantam · Mar 30 · 52 min

This episode explores the psychology of deception and challenges the universal moral principle that honesty is always the best policy. Host Shankar Vedantam and guest psychologist Emma Levine discuss research showing that people commonly accept lying in specific circumstances—when it spares vulnerable individuals unnecessary harm, involves subjective information, or protects sacred moments. Nick Capman (The FDA Group) · Mar 30 · 29 min

This episode discusses strategies for navigating global regulatory submissions across the FDA, EMA, and PMDA. AJ Acker, a 30-year industry veteran in rare disease development, explains that while these three major regulators share ICH harmonization standards, they interpret data through different regulatory lenses. The FDA prioritizes flexibility and speed, the EMA requires demonstrated meaningful benefit over existing therapies, and the PMDA traditionally demands Japanese data and feasibility evidence, though this is evolving. Key success factors include early regulatory engagement, alignment on clinical endpoints before Phase 3, use of local regulatory experts, and preparation for resource-intensive simultaneous filings.
