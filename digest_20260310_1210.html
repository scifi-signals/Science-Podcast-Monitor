MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: Sibling order may affect sexuality and identity
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Mar 10 · 12 min

This episode examines the fraternal birth order effect—a well-documented but still-mysterious pattern where gay men, and surprisingly gay women, tend to have more older brothers than other siblings. Scientists have studied this effect across numerous countries and proposed the maternal immune hypothesis as a biological explanation, where a mother's immune response to male fetus proteins may influence the sexuality of subsequent male children. However, recent large-scale research showing the effect also appears in women has complicated this hypothesis, leaving the underlying mechanism still unresolved. The episode also explores the troubling historical context of sexuality research, which began with eugenics-informed studies aimed at 'curing' homosexuality during a time when being queer was criminalized and dangerous.
