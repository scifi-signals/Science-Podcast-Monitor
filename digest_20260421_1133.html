MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: These voicemails save lives
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Apr 20 · 14 min

This episode explores how simple voicemail-based weather warning systems are saving lives in flood-prone Cambodia. Rebecca Hersher reports from the village of Prek Tush on the Mekong River, where residents now receive flood forecasts up to five days in advance via voicemail and local broadcast announcements. MEDIUM REACH Science Friday: How New Jersey tamped down PFAS in drinking water
Ira Flatow · Apr 20 · 12 min

New Jersey's 2018 drinking water standards for PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, or "forever chemicals") led to a 55% reduction in average PFAS concentrations in public water systems over approximately a decade, according to a Rutgers University study. However, researchers note that safe long-term exposure levels remain unclear, and the full health impacts of historical PFAS exposure are still being understood. The study highlights both the success of state-level regulation and ongoing scientific questions about PFAS accumulation in the body and links to kidney disease, liver dysfunction, and potential cancer risks. Other states are watching New Jersey's experience to develop their own PFAS policies.
