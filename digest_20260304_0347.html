MEDIUM REACH Nature Podcast: Audio long read: Many people have no mental imagery. What’s going on in their brains?
Springer Nature · Mar 02 · 18 min

This episode explores aphantasia, a neurological condition affecting approximately 4% of the population characterized by the absence or severe weakness of mental imagery. The episode traces the scientific discovery and study of aphantasia, beginning with neurologist Adam Zeman's 2015 paper that coined the term, and examines recent neuroscience research investigating how brains of people with aphantasia differ in visual cortex activity and inter-regional connectivity. Researchers have identified that aphantasia involves both an inability to consciously experience mental images and difficulties voluntarily generating them, despite the presence of visual representations in the brain. The condition appears to have genetic components, may be more common in technical professions, and notably reduces the vividness of autobiographical memories while having minimal impact on most behavioral and cognitive tasks.
