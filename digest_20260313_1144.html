MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: An icy mystery: What are lake stars?
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Mar 13 · 12 min

This episode explores lake stars, star-shaped patterns that form in ice when warm water percolates through slushy snow layers on frozen lakes. Geophysicist Victor Tsai from Brown University recreated these formations in a laboratory using a blender to make slush and carefully dripping warm water through it. His research has broader implications for understanding similar formations on Europa, Jupiter's icy moon, which could indicate the presence of subsurface liquid water and inform future exploration missions. The episode also discusses ice safety implications for people walking on frozen lakes.
