HIGH REACH Radiolab: Black Box
Lulu Miller, Latif Nasser · Feb 27 · 66 min

This episode explores three scientific and medical 'black boxes'—systems where inputs and outputs are visible but the mechanisms remain mysterious. The first examines how anesthesia works despite 170 years of clinical use, revealing that unconsciousness may result from disrupted neural communication through slow brain waves rather than global shutdown. The second segment is a rebroadcast about magicians Sydney and Lesley Piddington whose mind-reading act captivated 20 million listeners in the 1950s, with the mechanism remaining deliberately concealed. The final black box addresses metamorphosis in butterflies, explaining how caterpillars dissolve into cellular 'soup' within chrysalises yet somehow reorganize into butterflies, with evidence suggesting adult structures pre-exist within caterpillars.
