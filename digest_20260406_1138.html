MEDIUM REACH Short Wave: The world is running out of helium — again
Emily Kwong, Regina Barber · Apr 06 · 13 min

This episode explores helium's critical role in modern science and technology, tracing its discovery in 1868 through its current applications in MRI machines, quantum computers, and rocket engines. The episode highlights that helium is a finite resource on Earth—created slowly through radioactive decay of uranium and thorium—and that recent geopolitical tensions, particularly Iran's missile attacks on Qatar (the world's second-largest helium supplier), have triggered another helium shortage. The episode emphasizes that while helium balloons are often blamed for waste, the real pressure on helium supplies comes from scientific research and medical applications that require liquid helium for cooling superconductors.
